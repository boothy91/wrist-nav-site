How It Works

A walkthrough from opening the app to finishing your route

1
Download a map to your watch
Head to the Maps tab and tap Download Maps. Pick your country or region — some areas have a single map file, others are split into regions. Tap to download. Once done, maps are stored on the watch and work completely offline.
2
Import a GPX route (optional)
Got a route to follow? Import a GPX file via the Tracks tab from any file manager, Google Drive or email. You can also use the phone companion app to plan a route and send it directly to the watch over Wi-Fi. Tap the track to activate it for navigation.
3
Start recording from the home screen
The home screen shows your GPS signal strength. Toggle Nav On if you want to follow a route, then press Start. A countdown can be added in settings if you need a moment to get moving. Recording begins and the watch keeps itself active so GPS stays running.
4
Swipe through stats during your activity
Swipe between your stats pages while recording. Up to 5 pages, each fully customisable — choose which stats appear on each from Settings. Common setups are time, distance and pace on page one with heart rate, steps and elevation on page two.
5
Check the map at any time
Swipe past the last stats page to reach the map. Your position is shown live on the offline OSM map. If Nav is on, your GPX route is drawn on screen with remaining distance. The location button locks the map to follow you (red) or lets you drag freely (green). Slide the compass right to go back to stats.
6
Stop and save your recording
Press Stop from the home screen. Your route is saved as a GPX file on the watch. If the companion phone app is connected and in range, recordings are automatically sent to your phone — no manual transfer needed.
7
Upload, save or plan your next route
On the phone companion app, view received recordings, upload straight to Strava, or save the GPX to your phone. Use the built-in route planner to draw a new route on the map and send it to your watch ready for next time.
